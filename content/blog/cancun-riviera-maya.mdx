Cancún works when you treat it like a base, not just a stop.

## Start with the water

Use the first day for the easiest win: beach time, a slow lunch, and a short snorkel route near Isla Mujeres. That keeps the trip light and gives the rest of the itinerary room to breathe.

## Add one inland anchor

One cenote or one archaeological day is enough. The point is contrast: salt water, then fresh water, then a quiet dinner back on the coast.

## Keep transfers simple

The strongest version of this trip is not packed. It is paced:

- one high-energy excursion
- one recovery block
- one evening reserved for a premium dinner

That rhythm makes Cancún and the Riviera Maya feel like a clean luxury product instead of a crowded checklist.
