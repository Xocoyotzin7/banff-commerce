Mexico City is not one trip. It is several trips stacked in one grid.

## Start with a neighborhood

Choose one base and let it shape the day. Roma, Condesa, Coyoacán, and the Centro Histórico each give the city a different tone.

## Put food in the middle of the plan

A good route here is always built around food: market breakfast, a thoughtful lunch, and one dinner worth dressing up for.

## Leave space for a classic landmark

Teotihuacán, Xochimilco, or one major museum gives the trip a clear anchor. Without that, the city can feel exciting but directionless.

The premium version of Mexico City is not faster. It is cleaner, better paced, and easier to read.
